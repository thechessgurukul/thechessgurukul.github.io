The Boy Who Became India’s 92nd Grandmaster: A Story for Every Child

Once upon a time, there was a boy in India who loved chess. He didn't train in a massive academy or have famous coaches. He didn't even read thick, scary chess books. Yet, through simple dedication, Aaryan Varshney became India’s 92nd Chess Grandmaster in January 2026.

♟️ A Simple Beginning at Home
Aaryan’s journey started with his father, Gaurav Varshney, a physics teacher. They played together at home, focused on game analysis using software like ChessBase, and treated chess as a game to be loved, not a subject to be studied. It proves you don't need expensive mentors to reach the top!

Learning by Playing, Not by Fear

Aaryan's rise was incredibly fast—moving from International Master to Grandmaster in just six months! He shared a secret: he learned by playing and analyzing every game. Instead of feeling sad after a defeat, he would look at the board and ask: "What can I learn from this move?"

The Power of Belief

A huge part of Aaryan’s success was his father’s relentless support as a single parent. Even when they couldn't travel to Europe due to costs, they worked hard in India. This support created the confidence Aaryan needed to keep going, proving that belief can change everything.

5 Lessons for Young Players

✨ You don’t have to be in a big academy to begin.
✨ Learning from your own games is the best teacher.
✨ Losing is simply "data" to help you get better.
✨ Discipline and modern tools (like software) can replace expensive coaching.
✨ Big dreams grow from small daily efforts at home.

The next time you sit in front of a chessboard, remember:
Aaryan reached 2500 Elo without a coach.
If he can do it, your dreams are possible too!
